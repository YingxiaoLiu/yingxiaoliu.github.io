<p style="text-align: justify;">
Our research group investigates the multiscale creep behavior of shale through integrated experimental testing, material characterization, and constitutive modeling. We use nanoindentation creep tests to measure the local time-dependent response of shale constituents, including different mineral phases and organic-rich regions, and connect these measurements with microstructural and compositional information obtained from techniques such as X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy and X-ray diffraction.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
At the laboratory scale, we conduct triaxial creep tests to characterize the bulk deformation of shale under sustained loading and confinement. By bridging microscale nanoindentation measurements and macroscale triaxial creep behavior, we aim to understand how mineralogy, organic matter, anisotropy, pores, and microcracks govern long-term shale deformation.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
This project provides experimental data and physical insight for developing multiscale constitutive models that can predict the long-term mechanical response of shale in subsurface engineering applications, including energy extraction, underground storage, and carbon sequestration.
</p>
