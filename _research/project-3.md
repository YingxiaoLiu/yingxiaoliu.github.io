<p style="text-align: justify;">
Our research group explores the use of reinforcement learning to support decision-making and optimization in construction engineering. Construction projects involve many sequential decisions, including resource allocation, activity scheduling, equipment deployment, and response to changing site conditions. These decisions are often constrained by time, cost, safety, productivity, and uncertainty. Reinforcement learning provides a promising framework for learning adaptive strategies that can improve construction performance through repeated interaction with simulated project environments.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
We develop computational approaches that integrate construction process modeling with learning-based optimization. Instead of relying only on fixed rules or manually designed schedules, the reinforcement learning framework allows an agent to evaluate different decisions, learn from outcomes, and gradually identify strategies that improve project objectives. This approach is particularly useful for construction systems where decisions are interdependent, site conditions evolve over time, and optimal actions may change as the project progresses.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
The long-term goal of this work is to develop intelligent decision-support tools for construction planning and management. By combining engineering domain knowledge, simulation-based environments, and data-driven optimization, this research aims to improve the efficiency, adaptability, and resilience of construction operations while maintaining practical relevance for real-world project delivery.
</p>
