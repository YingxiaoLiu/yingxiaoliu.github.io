<h1 style="text-align: center; font-size: 32px;">
Computational Geomechanics for Resilient Infrastructure
</h1>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Welcome! I am Yingxiao Liu, an Assistant Professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Rowan University.
</p>

<p style="text-align: center;">
  <img src="/images/indentation.png" alt="indent" style="max-width: 600px; width: 100%; height: auto;"><br>
  <span style="font-size: 14px; font-style: italic;">
    FEM simulation of the nanoindentation creep test on a Woodford shale sample (Liu et al., 2023).
  </span>
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
My research focuses on computational geomechanics, geotechnical engineering, and the mechanics of porous geomaterials. I develop physics-based and data-driven models to understand how soils, rocks, and infrastructure materials behave under complex environmental and mechanical conditions, including freezing and thawing, creep, fracture, and coupled thermo-hydro-mechanical processes.
</p>

<p style="text-align: center;">
  <img src="/images/stage3end_flow.png" alt="flow_img" style="max-width: 600px; width: 100%; height: auto;"><br>
  <span style="font-size: 14px; font-style: italic;">
    Groundwater flow pattern and the pressure gradient contour during the artificial ground freezing (Liu & Sun, 2025).
  </span>
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
My broader goal is to advance resilient and sustainable infrastructure systems in response to climate change, energy demand, and natural hazards. Current research directions include freezing and thawing soils, permafrost degradation, multiscale modeling of shale and other geomaterials, fracture mechanics of infrastructure materials, and machine-learning-assisted computational modeling.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
In teaching and mentoring, I aim to connect fundamental theory with engineering practice through hands-on learning, computational tools, and open-ended problem solving. I teach courses in civil and environmental engineering, including fluid mechanics, geotechnical engineering, and engineering clinic projects.
</p>
